Mission:

OUR WORK

  1. Somatic Coregulation for Families, Couples & Groups
    In-person sessions held in nature for co-regulation, embodied healing, and integrative awareness. These sessions reject pathologizing models of health and center each participant in their inherent wisdom, sensory intelligence, and right to feel good. ‚ÄúYou are not broken. You are responding.   2. Living Infrastructure
    We facilitate cross-sector design conversations to replace toxic, rigid urban ground (like concrete sidewalks) with shock-absorbent, water-conductive, tree-root-sensitive surfaces. Our vision is walkable infrastructure that supports biomechanical health, environmental resilience, and rooted beauty.
  3. Regenerative Agriculture
    We advocate for a full transition from nutricidal to nutritious food systems‚Äîrestoring soil, plant, and human vitality while supporting Black, Indigenous, and place-based agricultural wisdom.
  4. Education Without Harm
    We reimagine learning environments by calling for the abolition of physical harm (including forced stillness and chronic sitting) as a basis for education. We support postural freedom, somatic learning, and sensory-responsive environments in schools.
  5. Environmental Remediation & Systems Debriefing
    We convene scientists, Indigenous land stewards, and affected communities to review and refine methods of remediation for toxic soil, air, and water. Our goal is coordinated, culturally accountable, ecologically sound responses to contamination and climate collapse.
  6. Rest & Mobility Innovation
    We develop ergonomic, somatically sound ‚Äúhuman consoles‚Äù for computing, rest, and transport‚Äîprototypes that free the body from lower spinal and pelvic immobilization of chairs and reclaim non-sitting forms of supported rest, navigation, and digital interface.
  7. Upgrade Building & Design Codes
    We advocate for the revision of architectural, interior, and product design standards to support lifelong neuromusculoskeletal health. Emphasis is placed on accommodating transverse plane mobility in the hip joints to prevent disablement from chronic overrotation, particularly when turning or walking around corners in confined spaces. We call for spatial layouts and circulation paths that allow for natural gait mechanics, with special attention to the femoroacetabular joint.

    We also promote ergonomic reforms that normalize adjustable surface heights in domestic, educational, and workplace environments‚Äîensuring that people of varying statures can interact safely with furniture, fixtures, and tools. This includes elevating common appliances such as ovens and dishwashers to hip height or higher, and eliminating drawers or shelving systems positioned below the hip, which require repeated forward flexion and torque at vulnerable angles. Standards should also reflect inclusive height clearances, including ceiling fans and lighting fixtures that do not pose collision risks for taller individuals.
